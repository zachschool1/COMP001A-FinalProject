Introduction

Nearly 70% of adults in the United States are overweight or obese. Overweight and Obesity are associated with a number of chornic diseases, including type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease. Even modest weight loss, described as 5% decrease in body weight, has been shown to lower the risk of chronic disease. Many people are turning to Plant-Based Diets (PBDs) for weight loss.

PBDs, including vegan and vegetarian diets, are based around fruits, vegetables, grains, and legumes, with vegetarian diets also typically including dairy products and eggs.

Vegetarian diet

Vegetarian diets do not contain meat, fish or poultry, but they do typically contain eggs and dairy, in addition to plant-based foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes/beans.

An example dinner meal would be something like Black-eyed peas and brown rice with chopped tomatoes, roasted peppers, and reduced fat cheddar cheese with a side salad.

Pescatarian (or Presco-Vegetarian)

Pescatarian meals do not contain meat or poultry but they do contain fish and shellfish, eggs, and dairy, in addition to plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes/beans

an example dinner meal would be something like Black-eyed peas and brown rice with shrimp, chopped tomatoes, roasted peppers, and reduced fat cheddar cheese with a side salad.

Vegan

a Vegan diet Does not contain any animal products (meat, fish, poultry, eggs, or dairy) but emphasizes plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes/beans.

An example dinner meal would be something like Black-eyed peas and brown rice with chopped tomatoes and roasted peppers with a side salad.

Semi-Vegetarian

a semi-vegetarian diet Contains all foods, including meat, poultry, fish and shellfish, eggs, and dairy, in addition to plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes/beans. However, red meat and poultry are fairly limited.

A dinner meal example would be Black-eyed peas and brown rice with low-fat turkey sausage, chopped tomatoes, roasted peppers, and reduced fat cheddar cheese with a side salad.

Omnivorous

an omnivorous diet is something most people are familiar with. teses diets contain all food groups with no real restrictions. There are a lot of options with an omnivorous diet, but it can lead to issues if you do not keep track of what you are doing, as you can possibly not fulfil all aspects of a proper diet.

An example meal would be something like Black-eyed peas and brown rice with low-fat sausage, chopped tomatoes, roasted peppers, and reduced fat cheddar cheese with, of course, a side salad.
